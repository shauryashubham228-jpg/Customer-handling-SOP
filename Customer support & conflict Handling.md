

## 💡 Customer Support: FAQ & Conflict Resolution

This section is designed for the Customer Care team to standardize responses and handle high-stress situations.

### 2. Failure Handling Protocol

*Use this when a process goes wrong (e.g., lost package, delayed shipment).*

- **Acknowledge & Validate:** Immediately apologize for the inconvenience without being defensive.
    - *Script:* "I sincerely apologize for the delay. I understand how frustrating it is to wait for your order."
- **Root Cause Analysis (Internal):** Before replying to the customer, check the internal status:
    - Is it a logistics partner delay?
    - Is it a packaging/inventory hold?
    - *Action:* Provide the customer with the specific reason and a **new, realistic ETA**.
- **Resolution Options:**
    - **If Delayed:** Offer a discount coupon for the next purchase to maintain goodwill.
    - **If Lost/Damaged:** Initiate an immediate replacement order or offer a full store credit refund.
    - **Closure:** Never leave a ticket open. Once resolved, send a summary message confirming the resolution.

### 3. Stress Handling (Agent Guidelines)

*Handling escalated or aggressive customer interactions.*

- **The "DE-ESCALATE" Rule:**
    1. **D**etach: Do not take the customer’s frustration personally. They are upset about the service, not you.
    2. **E**mpathize: Use phrases like, "I would be equally concerned if I were in your position."
    3. **A**ct: Move immediately to the solution. "Here is exactly what I am going to do to fix this for you right now."
    4. **L**isten: Allow the customer to vent briefly; often, they just want to be heard before they will accept a solution.
- **Escalation Threshold:**
    - If a customer uses abusive language, politely warn them once: *"I want to help you resolve this, but I must ask you to remain professional so we can continue our conversation."*
    - If they persist, escalate to the Team Lead immediately. Do not engage in an argument.
- **Documentation:** Always log the details of the stress interaction in the CRM/Notion log to track recurring issues with specific logistics partners or product lines.
